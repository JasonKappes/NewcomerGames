Privacy Policy

Last Updated: December 20, 2024

Welcome to Wisp, a desktop application developed by Newcomer Inc. This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our application. Please read this policy carefully to understand our views and practices regarding your personal data and how we will treat it.

1. Introduction

Newcomer Inc. ("we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy applies to your use of Wisp ("Service"), including any data you provide while using the application. By using Wisp, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

2. Information We Collect

a. Personal Information

Email Address (Optional):
Users may choose to provide their email address when opting to create an account. This information is used solely for account-related communications and support.

b. Non-Personal Information

Usage Information:
We collect non-personal usage data that includes information on how frequently users interact with "Wisps" to engage with in-game text. This helps us understand usage patterns and improve the Service.

Screenshot Data:
When users utilize the Shine or Stream Wisp features, Wisp captures screenshots of the active window. These screenshots are processed to provide translations, definitions, explanations, and pronunciations of in-game text.

3. How We Use Your Information

To Provide and Improve the Service:
Usage data helps us enhance Wisp's functionality and user experience. Screenshot data is used to deliver accurate language learning assistance within video games.

Communication:
If you provide your email address, we may use it to send you important updates, support information, or respond to your inquiries.

Research and Development:
We may share anonymized usage data with university researchers to gain insights into language learning methodologies and further improve Wisp.

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure

Legal Requirements:
We may disclose your information if required to do so by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities.

5. Data Storage and Security
